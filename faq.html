FAQ / Help

Simple answers for busy households who want to understand their electricity use quickly.

How do I know if my usage is normal, high, or low?

EnergyCheck uses simple colours to show your electricity usage status. Green means your usage is normal, amber means it is higher than usual, and red means it is very high.

Tip: Look at the colour first, then read the short explanation under the chart. This helps you understand the main message quickly.

Why is my electricity bill higher than usual?

A higher bill can happen for many reasons. Common causes include air conditioning, heating, dryers, cooking appliances, older appliances, or more people staying at home.

A high bill does not always mean you did something wrong. Energy use can change because of weather, household size, appliance age, or daily routines.

Why compare my usage with similar households?

Different households use electricity in different ways. A small apartment and a large family home should not be compared in the same way.

EnergyCheck compares your usage with similar households so the result is fairer and easier to understand.

What can I control?

You can control some daily habits, even if you cannot change the building or appliances.

  • Turn off lights when leaving a room.
  • Use fans before turning on air conditioning.
  • Wash clothes with full loads.
  • Unplug devices when they are not being used.
  • Use natural light during the day when possible.

What can’t I control as a renter?

Renters may have limited control over some energy-related problems. For example, you may not be able to change insulation, fixed appliances, solar panels, windows, or building design.

EnergyCheck focuses on realistic and affordable actions, so renters can still make small changes without needing landlord approval.

Do I need to log in to use EnergyCheck?

No. You can access the basic information, comparison, and energy-saving advice without creating an account or logging in.

This makes the website quicker and easier to use for people with limited time.

How can I quickly reduce my electricity use?

Start with simple actions that do not cost money. Small habits can still make a difference over time.

  • Set air conditioning to a moderate temperature.
  • Switch off standby power at the wall.
  • Use cold water for washing when possible.
  • Dry clothes naturally instead of using a dryer.
  • Check the Comparison page once a month.
