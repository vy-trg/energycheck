Control

Learn what affects your electricity bills and what actions you can take to reduce energy usage.

What You Can Control vs What You Can't Control

✅ Things You Can Control

  • Turning lights off when not in use
  • Using fans before air conditioning
  • Turning appliances off at the wall
  • Washing clothes during off-peak hours
  • Closing blinds during hot afternoons

⛔ Things You Cannot Control

  • Weather and outdoor temperatures
  • Old appliances provided by landlords
  • Poor insulation in apartments
  • Shared electricity systems
  • Building design and sun exposure

Quick Actions

Small changes every day can help lower electricity costs.

  • Set air conditioning to 24°C
  • Unplug chargers when not needed
  • Use natural sunlight during the day
  • Switch devices off before sleeping
  • Avoid leaving appliances on standby

Why Is My Bill Higher Than Usual?

A higher bill does not always mean you did something wrong. Common reasons include:

  • More heating or cooling due to seasonal temperature changes
  • More people staying at home than usual
  • Older appliances provided by your landlord using more energy
  • Leaving devices on standby for longer periods
  • Running appliances during peak electricity hours

Focus on the things you can control. Small changes can still reduce your electricity bills and energy usage.
